Est 2003

From a cellar in Manchester, a spare bedroom* north of Preston and a terraced* house in York, we came together to establish a creative studio called Wash. Today, we have a national reputation for producing engaging and effective visual communications.

They were humble beginnings, back in 2003. For as long as agencies have existed, when new ones started up, they usually had a client or two to get started on. Not us. We had nothing. Just an idea, a pedigree and a work ethic.

Andrew Shaw had been a marketing manager with Baird Menswear Group, dealing with brands like Lowe Alpine and Van Gils, and an Account Executive with Saatchi & Saatchi before that. He soon became the face of Wash.

Dan, Andrews’ brother, had worked in Events & Moving Image, working with clients like PWC and Royal Bank of Scotland. He soon got into web and Flash development, becoming the driving force behind our digital engagement.

Andy Walmsley already had 20 years’ studio experience by the time Wash got going. Having started out a local publication through a Youth Opportunities Scheme, he worked in various agencies of all shapes and sizes. Good training for becoming our Creative Director.

Before we could get to work, the studio needed a name. Something short. Memorable, but everyday. Wash was here. It’s cleansing: out with the old, in with the new. It might have started out with the first letters of our surnames, but the rationale soon came – as it so often does (and don’t let anyone tell you otherwise).

An Evolution

From the start, we aspired to deliver the highest standards of creativity. Maybe Lancashire wasn’t the easiest place to start out – we worried that most of the talent haemorrhaged to London or Manchester, and that many of the clients who valued creativity did the same. But we challenged that culture then and we still do today.

In 2008, Andrew left the agency to move to Finland with his family. It was a difficult period and the recession hit us hard. But our direction and approach never faltered. We kept on going, building the team, adding great people that shared our passion and ethos.

We’re down to earth, friendly and honest. Straight-talking Northern folk. We’re at home on the fells, or on our bikes. We don’t schmooze clients at golf courses or cocktail bars (unless they ask for that). But we might just treat you to a pint in our local or a plate of goodness from our favourite chippy.

Since we opened our doors, visual communication and brand marketing has seen an incredible degree of change – and it’s still developing at an ever-increasing pace. We’ve evolved too, making sure we’re ready for what’s next and, in many cases, keeping a step ahead of the industry.

That’s why we knew it was time for a change in 2009. Instead of positioning ourselves as a ‘Boutique Design Practice’, we put ourselves at the centre of a wide-ranging network of experts: we turned Wash into a Visual Communication Agency ready to transform our clients inside and out.

We work hard to explore what’s happening around us – attending events, lectures and exhibitions in the UK, across Europe and, if the budget allows, anywhere else in the world. We even run our own events under the title ‘Conversation in Creativity’, supported by Creative Lancashire.

Roots

Our satellite base in London, Shoreditch, opened in summer 2013. London is buzzing once again and the digital evolution is gaining even more momentum. It was a good strategic move to be there – by having a digital team working out of a studio shared with App Creators, Software Developers, Social Marketing Experts, UX Specialists, Content Strategists and many others.

There are things going on there that really are ahead of the game, and we’re part of it, we can bring that knowledge into our team –  and ultimately to our clients.

We will always stay true to our roots, but we will always keep evolving. It’s what we do and how we keep our clients in touch with the latest marketing thinking. Our team is devoted to working with clients to make better brands.

So before you allow a creative agency to work on your Brand &:amp; Visual Communications, no matter how big or small the project, you need to be certain of a few things; they must have the kind of ability and experience that inspires, they must be the kind of people you’ll get on with and they must be able to produce the results you need.

That’s our story. We hope it helps you decide how to tell yours.
